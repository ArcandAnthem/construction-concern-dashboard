Executive Summary

July strategy should not be built around the assumption that social is driving discovery or site action. The strongest current signals are direct traffic, Google organic search, and one clearly proven non-brand content lane: fire safety and boxed-eaves education. The best-performing owned web asset remains the boxed-eaves fire-safety post, while the Contact and About pages continue to outperform most service pages.

June also clarified what not to overstate. LinkedIn published 11 posts for 346 total impressions and zero measurable engagement. Instagram generated 243 total views, 73 total reach, and a net -1 follower change. Threads remained low volume. Email delivered but did not produce strong click behavior in the visible June export. The July posture should therefore be pragmatic: social supports recognition and saves, while website behavior and search demand determine the priority topics.

Operating Diagnosis
Discovery Channel Mismatch

Social is not the main discovery engine. Direct traffic and Google organic search are doing materially more work than social referral.

Proven Topic Lane

Boxed eaves, fire protection, soffits, and related exterior-protection education remain the clearest non-brand content winners.

Trust-Page Behavior

Contact and About are outperforming most service pages, which suggests the audience is validating the company before committing to estimate action.

Platform Ceiling

LinkedIn engagement is effectively zero, Instagram is flat to down, Threads is low volume, and social overall should be treated as a support layer instead of a primary traffic engine.
