## Status

## Posting strategy for the month

May content focuses on **practical bundled solutions that build trust through education and transparent process clarity**. Construction Concern's posts will emphasize real proof (before/afters, team stories, clean installs), address customer objections transparently (timelines, warranties, communication), and position the company as the trusted local alternative to larger franchises. Posts rotate between three audience angles: ROI-focused (energy savings, durability, cost efficiency), lifestyle-focused (comfort, design, daily improvement), and practical preparation (spring-to-summer readiness, local relevance). All posts name specific geographies (Santa Clarita, Los Angeles, San Fernando Valley) to improve SEO and reinforce local expertise.

The posting mix is distributed across LinkedIn (2), Facebook (5), Instagram (3), and Threads (2) to match audience behavior and platform strengths. LinkedIn emphasizes leadership and team credibility, Facebook drives community trust and local reference, Instagram showcases before/afters and visual proof, and Threads offers brief educational moments and quick process tips.

## Research guiding the posting strategy

- Construction Concern's 35+ years of referral-driven local trust and bundled service breadth are the clearest differentiators against scale competitors like American Vision Windows and Window World.
- Customer reviews show strong proof points (clean installs, knowledgeable crews) but reveal friction around scheduling clarity, warranty follow-through, and communication recovery. Posts address these transparently through process education.
- Audience segmentation shows a split between budget optimizers (energy ROI, durability), lifestyle improvers (comfort, design, aesthetics), and decision-makers (bundled solutions, operational value). May content alternates between these angles.
- May is peak spring planning season for window replacement and HVAC decisions, with timely local relevance around wildfire safety, summer heat prep, and energy costs in Santa Clarita/LA/Ventura markets.
- Website audit gaps (missing blog, testimonials, FAQs) suggest social posts should support discoverability, build trust assets, and drive traffic to estimate requests.
