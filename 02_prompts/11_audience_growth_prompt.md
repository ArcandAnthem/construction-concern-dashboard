# Audience Growth Prompt

You are generating a monthly community engagement and audience growth plan for Construction Concern.

## Purpose

Publishing content alone does not grow a following. Algorithmic platforms reward accounts that actively engage — not just broadcast. This prompt builds a monthly plan for earning new followers, increasing engagement signals, and building local community presence without paid advertising.

The goal is not to manufacture fake engagement. It is to show up consistently in the spaces where Construction Concern's ideal audience (35–44 homeowners in LA County and Ventura County) already exists.

## Inputs

Add to context before running this prompt:
- `01_config/client_profile.md`
- the monthly analytics review findings (from `08_analytics_review_prompt.md`)
- the monthly social strategy

## The core principle

Before anyone follows you, they have to notice you. They notice you when you show up usefully in their feed, their community, or their search results — not just in your own posts.

## Monthly engagement activities

Generate a specific monthly engagement plan that includes the following:

### 1. Comment engagement (Instagram and Facebook)
Identify 3–5 types of accounts to actively engage with each week by leaving thoughtful, useful comments:
- Local Santa Clarita / Newhall / Simi Valley neighborhood accounts
- Local real estate agents and property managers
- Home improvement inspiration accounts (not direct competitors)
- Local news pages covering LA County homeowner topics (fire safety, LADWP, insurance)
- Community Facebook groups (participation only — no promotional posts)

For each, suggest a comment approach that adds value without selling.

### 2. Follow strategy
Identify categories of accounts to follow each month to signal to the algorithm what community Construction Concern belongs to:
- Local homeowner groups
- SoCal real estate professionals
- Local neighborhood Instagram accounts
- Home improvement educators (national, non-competing)

### 3. Facebook group participation
List 2–3 specific types of local Facebook groups to join and participate in. Provide guidance on how to answer questions helpfully without triggering spam filters (no links in first replies, be a neighbor not a salesperson).

### 4. Story and interactive content (Instagram)
Suggest 2 interactive Story posts per month to drive direct engagement:
- Poll stickers ("Which would you fix first: windows or roof?")
- Question stickers ("What's the #1 home project you've been putting off?")
- "This or That" graphics (casement vs. double-hung windows, etc.)

### 5. Response protocol
Provide guidance for responding to any comments or DMs within 24 hours:
- Acknowledge, don't just like
- Ask a follow-up question when appropriate
- Never argue or get defensive
- Keep a warm, helpful tone consistent with the brand voice

### 6. Hashtag strategy review
Review and update the monthly hashtag set to balance:
- Local tags (e.g., #NewhallCA #SantaClaritaCA #LosAngelesHomeowners)
- Service tags (e.g., #WindowReplacement #RoofingContractor #SidingInstallation)
- Audience tags (e.g., #HomeImprovement #HomeMaintenance #SoCalHomes)
- Avoid oversaturated tags over 10M posts — they bury your content

## Rules

1. All engagement must be authentic — do not recommend follow/unfollow tactics or engagement pods.
2. Facebook group participation: never post promotional content in groups without explicit permission.
3. Do not tag competitors or leave comments that compare.
4. Hashtag sets must be reviewed monthly — do not reuse the same set indefinitely.
5. Engagement activities must be realistic for a small business operator — aim for 15 minutes per day maximum.
6. Growth targets should be framed as directional, not guaranteed.
