# Hook Writing Prompt

You are rewriting or generating scroll-stopping first lines for Construction Concern social posts.

## Purpose

The hook is the first line of any social post. It is the only line that determines whether someone stops scrolling or keeps going. All other copy is wasted if the hook fails.

Data from Construction Concern's own analytics shows that list-format hooks (e.g., "3 signs your windows may be costing you comfort") dramatically outperform lifestyle or brand-statement openers.

## Inputs

Add to context before running this prompt:
- `01_config/client_profile.md`
- any draft captions that need hooks rewritten
- the monthly research summary (for current topics and local context)

## Hook formats that work for this audience (35–44 homeowners, LA/Ventura County)

Use these proven formats:

**List hooks**
- "3 signs your [home element] is ready to be replaced"
- "5 things homeowners in [local area] don't know about [topic]"
- "4 reasons your [problem] keeps coming back"

**Problem/pain hooks**
- "Your [home element] might be costing you more than you think."
- "Most homeowners wait too long on this — and it costs them."
- "If your [problem], you're not alone. Here's what's usually causing it."

**Question hooks**
- "When's the last time you looked at your [home element]?"
- "How do you know when it's time to replace vs. repair?"
- "What would you fix first — windows or roof?"

**Local/seasonal relevance hooks**
- "With [season] hitting the [region], this is the one thing to check first."
- "Here in [local area], [home problem] is one of the most common calls we get."
- "Fire season in SoCal means one thing for homeowners: your eaves matter."

**Myth-busting hooks**
- "Most people think [common belief]. Here's what's actually true."
- "If someone tells you [bad advice], get a second opinion."
- "The cheapest fix isn't always the cheapest fix."

**Transparency/trust hooks**
- "Here's what a free estimate actually includes — and what it doesn't."
- "We're going to tell you the truth about [common service] pricing."

## Hook rules

1. The first line must stand alone — it must make sense and create curiosity without reading the rest.
2. Never start with the company name or "we."
3. Never start with a generic greeting ("Happy Monday," "Spring is here").
4. Never start with a boast ("We're proud to," "Construction Concern is excited to").
5. Keep it under 12 words when possible.
6. Write it for someone mid-scroll on a phone — they skim, they don't read.
7. The hook must connect to a real homeowner pain point, question, or curiosity — not a brand message.

- When adapting a topic that hasn't been posted about before
