## Newsletter #1

**Subject Line:** 5 things to check before your LADWP bill peaks
**Preview Text:** A quick June checklist for Santa Clarita and LA County homeowners.

---

June is when LADWP bills start to climb.

If your home isn't efficient — even a little — the difference can be $50–100 a month in additional cooling costs. The good news is that most of the biggest drivers are fixable.

Here's what to look at this month before the heat peaks.

---

**Your June Home Energy Checklist**

✅ **Check your window seals.**
Run your hand around the frame on a warm afternoon. If you feel heat coming through the glass or a gap at the edges, your window isn't doing its job. Older single-pane windows can account for 25–30% of a room's heat gain in summer.

✅ **Look at your HVAC filter.**
If you haven't changed it since spring, do it now. A clogged filter forces your system to work harder — and when it's working harder in June, you feel it in July's bill.

✅ **Check your soffits and attic vents.**
Trapped heat in an attic pushes down into the living space and makes your AC work overtime. Make sure attic vents are clear and unobstructed.

✅ **Walk your exterior.**
Look for cracks, gaps, or worn caulking around windows, doors, and exterior trim. Small gaps create energy loss — and in June, also create pathways for embers if you're in a fire-adjacent area.

✅ **Ask about rebates before you do anything.**
LADWP offers rebates for qualifying window replacements and HVAC upgrades. Most homeowners don't know to ask. Ask us about rebate eligibility when you call.

---

**Quick tip:** The best time to book summer home improvement work is before it gets hot. Projects started in June or early July are typically completed before peak August heat.

## Newsletter #2

**Subject Line:** 4 exterior upgrades before fire season peaks
**Preview Text:** What your home's exterior should have this summer in SoCal.

---

Fire season is open in Southern California.

If you're in the Santa Clarita Valley, Newhall, Simi Valley, or anywhere in the LA and Ventura County foothills — you already know what that means. The risk is real, and most of the most important exterior preparations are also improvements that add long-term value to your home.

Here's what to look at.

---

**Protecting Your Home's Exterior Before Fire Season Peaks**

Construction Concern has served the LA and Ventura County area for 35+ years. We work on the exact parts of a home's exterior that matter most during fire season.

**Soffits and eaves — where most homes are most vulnerable.**
Open eaves and unenclosed soffits are the most common points of ember entry in residential fires. Fire-resistant or enclosed soffits are one of the most impactful upgrades a homeowner in this area can make.

**Siding material matters.**
Wood siding and certain engineered materials have significantly lower fire resistance ratings than modern fiber cement or stucco options. If your siding is older or wood-based, it's worth a conversation.

**Vents and screens.**
Ember-resistant vent screens are inexpensive but often overlooked. Under-eave vents, attic vents, and crawl space vents should be fitted with ember-resistant screens.

**Seal and protect exterior trim and gaps.**
Even small gaps in exterior trim, around windows, and at foundation edges are pathways for embers and heat. Caulking and sealing these areas is a low-cost, high-impact step.

---

**Quick tip:** CAL FIRE recommends having your roof, eaves, and exterior vents inspected annually before fire season. If you haven't had a professional walk-through, this is the right time.
