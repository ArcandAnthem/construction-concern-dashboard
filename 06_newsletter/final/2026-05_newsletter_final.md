## Newsletter #1

**Recommended Send Window:** Early May (May 2-5)  
**Subject Line:** Why May Is Smart for Window Upgrades
**Preview Text:** Before summer heat hits, here's what homeowners should know.

**Opening:**  
Spring in Southern California is when the weather changes fast. It's also when window decisions become practical. If you've been thinking about upgrades, May is actually the ideal month to start planning—before summer energy costs climb and contractors get busier.

**Primary Feature:**  
**Why Window Upgrades Make Sense in May**

**Energy costs.** Summer cooling expenses in Los Angeles typically increase significantly from spring levels. Homes with older windows feel that right away. New windows with better insulation reduce the load on your AC system and lower your bills.

**Scheduling advantage.** Contractors have more availability in May and early June. Planning now means you can schedule work before the peak summer rush, often with faster turnaround.

**Comfort.** It's not just about the bill. Better insulation means more consistent interior temperatures, less reliance on turning up AC, and a noticeably more comfortable home during heat waves.

**Curb appeal.** New windows refresh how your home looks and feels—something you'll appreciate every time you come home, especially going into summer.

**Local considerations.** For Santa Clarita and LA-area homes, fire-resistant window options add an extra layer of exterior safety. Spring is a good time to evaluate whether that makes sense for your property.

**Quick Tip:**  
Compare windows by these measures: energy ratings (look for ENERGY STAR certification), sound reduction (helpful if you're near traffic), and style (make sure the frames fit your home). Don't let price be the only factor—durability matters.

You get a thorough site inspection from someone who knows the work. We look at your current windows, siding, HVAC, or solar situation, listen to your goals—whether that's energy savings, comfort, design, or budget—and explain realistic options. No assumptions. No surprise discoveries later.
