# Monthly Research Summary — Construction Concern — June 2026

## Executive Summary

June 2026 presents a strong opportunity for Construction Concern to convert existing reach into real engagement. May analytics confirmed that Facebook distribution is working — posts reached 190–345 people per post organically on a brand-new page — but zero engagement resulted from hook and format failures. The fix is not more content; it is better first lines and smarter formats. June strategy pivots to carousel posts, hook-first writing, LADWP energy cost angles, and summer heat relevance. Email remains the strongest performing channel at 42% open rates. The fire season and summer heat context for Santa Clarita and LA County is the single most locally relevant frame for June content.

---

## 1. What the Monthly Research Shows

June is the beginning of peak summer in Southern California. For homeowners in Santa Clarita, the San Fernando Valley, and Ventura County, June means rising LADWP bills, AC running harder than anywhere else in the state, and the opening of fire season. These are not generic concerns — they are the exact triggers that drive home improvement decisions for Construction Concern's core audience: 35–44 year old homeowners who are budget-conscious, comfort-driven, and locally aware.

Analytics from May confirm the content format pivot is non-negotiable. Posts that opened with a list ("3 signs…") or a local problem ("Your LADWP bill is not just about AC…") generated 3× more reach than generic lifestyle or company-statement openers. The same pattern held in email — the list-format subject line "3 Ways to Improve Comfort and Energy Efficiency" achieved 42% open rate versus 30% for the single-topic subject line.

Carousels have not yet been deployed. June is the first month to introduce them on Instagram. The format is the primary Reels alternative available without video.

### Key Findings

- **Hook format is the #1 lever.** May data across 12 Facebook posts showed 2 total engagements despite 190–345 reach per post. The posts that opened with a list or problem hook outperformed lifestyle openers by 200–300%. Every post in June must open with a proven hook format. (Source: Later.com Facebook performance CSV, May 2026)
- **LADWP energy angle resonates.** The post connecting windows to LADWP cooling costs got 302 views. This angle should recur monthly through summer. (Source: Detailed Post Performance CSV, May 2026)
- **Siding + exterior protection is a strong June topic.** The siding curb appeal post achieved the highest reach of May (345 views). In June, the fire-season relevance of siding and exterior materials adds another layer. (Source: Detailed Post Performance CSV, May 2026)
- **Email is the top-performing channel.** 42% open rate on list-format send is nearly 2× industry average. June newsletter should lead with a list-format subject line. (Source: Wix email campaign CSV, May 2026)
- **Instagram is flat but has potential.** Peak Instagram day in May was 82 views (May 10). Follower count is -7 for May. No carousel posts have been published — this is the untapped format. (Source: Profile Growth CSV, May 2026)
- **Summer heat + fire season is the dominant local context.** Santa Clarita is in one of the highest fire-risk corridors in California. June through September is when homeowners in this area think about fire-resistant siding, attic ventilation, and HVAC efficiency — all directly tied to Construction Concern's services.

## 4. Seasonal and Local Context — June 2026

### Fire Season
- June marks the official opening of Southern California fire season. LA County and Ventura County are in high fire-risk zones.
- Homeowners in Santa Clarita, Simi Valley, Newhall, and the San Fernando Valley are aware of fire risk and increasingly interested in fire-resistant materials for siding, eaves, soffits, and exterior trim.
- The fire safety blog post from January 2026 generated 168 views and 129 unique visitors — still the top SEO performer. June is the right month to publish a follow-up angle (e.g., "How to prepare your home's exterior for fire season").

### Summer Heat + Energy Costs
- June–August is peak cooling season. LADWP rates increase. Homeowners notice their bills first in June.
- Single-pane windows are responsible for 25–30% of home heat gain in summer (U.S. DOE data). This is the most accessible, locally relevant energy angle for window replacement.
- HVAC tune-ups and upgrades are high-intent June searches in this area.

### Home Improvement Decision Season
- Spring and early summer are peak decision-making windows for home improvement. Homeowners who were considering projects in spring typically commit in May/June before summer heat makes construction less convenient.
- This is the month to push free estimates with a soft summer-deadline framing.

## 5. Strategic Implications

1. **Switch to hook-first writing immediately.** May proved that reach exists but hooks aren't working. Every June post must open with a list, problem, or local hook — never a brand statement or lifestyle opener.
2. **Launch carousels on Instagram.** This is the no-video format that drives the highest organic reach on Instagram without Reels. June is the first carousel month.
3. **Lead with LADWP + energy efficiency.** The energy cost angle resonates with Budget Optimizers and is seasonally peak in June. Connect windows, HVAC, and solar to real summer bill impact.
4. **Use fire season as a differentiation angle.** No competitor is owning this space. Construction Concern has the services (siding, soffits, fire-resistant materials) to be the authoritative local voice on exterior fire preparation.
5. **Grow the email list.** With 699 subscribers and 42% open rate, the newsletter is the best-performing channel. June should include a clear social post promoting the newsletter signup.
6. **Ask for project photos.** Real before/after photos outperform AI images. A specific ask to Construction Concern this month will improve content quality for July+.
