# Internal Research Dashboard — Construction Concern — June 2026

## Executive Summary

May analytics confirmed the core diagnosis: reach exists, engagement does not. Facebook reached 190–345 people per post on a brand-new page — that is strong organic distribution. But 12 posts generated only 2 total engagements. This is a hook and format failure, not a distribution failure. June strategy pivots to hook-first writing, carousel format on Instagram, and doubling down on the LADWP energy and fire season angles that demonstrated clear reach advantage in May. Email remains the strongest channel at 42% open rate. The newsletter list is at 699 — growing it is the second priority after fixing engagement.

## 2. What the Monthly Research Shows

June is peak summer onset in Southern California. LADWP bills increase. Fire season opens officially. Homeowners in Santa Clarita, Newhall, and the San Fernando Valley are actively thinking about cooling costs and fire risk — both of which tie directly to Construction Concern's core services (windows, HVAC, siding, solar, soffits).

### Key Findings
- **Hook failure is the root cause of zero engagement.** May posts with lifestyle openers ("A kitchen can feel updated…", "Sometimes a home does not need a complete overhaul…") achieved near-zero engagement despite 200+ reach. Posts with problem or local hooks (LADWP windows post, siding curb appeal) achieved 280–345 reach. The difference is the first line. (Source: Detailed_Post_Performance CSV, May 2026)
- **LADWP energy angle is the strongest June hook.** The LADWP/windows energy cost post got 302 views — third highest of all May posts. Summer billing season peaks in June. This angle recurs monthly through August. (Source: post data, May 2026)
- **Siding + exterior reached the most people.** 345 views for siding curb appeal post. Fire season adds a second reason to post about exterior materials in June. (Source: post data, May 2026)
- **Email is the best-performing channel.** 42% open rate on list-format subject line — nearly 2× industry benchmark of ~22%. The list is growing (555 → 699 recipients across two sends). (Source: Wix email CSV, May 2026)
- **Carousels = untapped format.** Zero carousels have been published on Instagram. This is the primary Reels alternative available. Launching in June.
- **Fire safety blog drives sustainable SEO traffic.** 168 views since January from search — highest single-page performer. A June follow-up (fire season exterior prep) can capture the same search intent. (Source: blog data, prior months)

### Why It Matters
Construction Concern has working distribution — posts are reaching people. The engagement problem is entirely solvable with better first lines and smarter formats. June is the highest-stakes month for this pivot because fire season and LADWP billing create genuine homeowner urgency. If content connects to those concerns with a sharp hook, engagement should improve materially.

## 5. Seasonal and Local Context — June 2026

- **Fire season:** CAL FIRE typically activates enhanced preparedness protocols in early June. Santa Clarita, Newhall, Simi Valley, and Ventura County are within high-risk corridors. Homeowners are actively researching fire-resistant siding, soffit venting, and exterior protection. Construction Concern has direct service relevance.
- **LADWP summer rates:** Summer billing increases affect all LA County customers. Posts connecting window performance or HVAC efficiency to LADWP bill reduction are highly search-relevant in June.
- **Peak heat onset:** June marks the beginning of 90°F+ days in the Santa Clarita Valley. Comfort-driven homeowners feel the urgency now.
- **Project timing window:** Homeowners who decide in June can have work completed before peak July/August heat. This is a legitimate urgency frame.

## 6. Strategic Recommendations

1. **Apply hook-first writing to every June post.** Zero exceptions. Test: does the first line stop a scroll on a phone? If not, rewrite it before scheduling.
2. **Launch carousel format on Instagram.** Minimum 3 carousel posts in June. Each carousel: 4–6 slides, one point per slide, list or step format.
3. **Lead with LADWP + energy cost angle.** This is the sharpest June hook. Connects windows, HVAC, and solar to a real bill the audience receives this month.
4. **Publish fire season exterior prep content.** Blog post + matching social posts. Construction Concern is the right local voice for this. No competitor is owning it.
5. **Use question/poll posts on Facebook.** Two question-format posts in June designed to generate comments. Example: "Which runs up your LADWP bill more — AC or old windows? Drop your answer below."
6. **Promote the newsletter signup on social.** One post in June directing followers to sign up. List is at 699 — goal is 1,000.
7. **Ask Construction Concern for project photos.** Before/after photos from recent jobs are the single highest-impact visual asset available. Include as a client action item.

### Risks / Watchouts
- **Unsubscribe spike on Email 2 (21 unsubs).** This is higher than healthy (0.5% of list = ~3–4 expected). Monitor June send. If it continues, the list may include non-engaged signups who need a re-engagement sequence or list cleaning. Frequency and relevance should be reviewed.
- **Continued zero engagement.** If June posts still achieve 0 engagement despite hook changes, the issue may be post timing or platform algorithm limits for new pages — not content. Will flag at July review.
