# Internal Research Dashboard - Construction Concern - July 2026

## Executive Summary
July strategy should not be built around the assumption that social is driving discovery or site action. The strongest current signals are direct traffic, Google organic search, and one clearly proven non-brand content lane: fire safety and boxed-eaves education. The best-performing owned web asset remains https://www.constructionconcern.com/post/boxed-in-eaves-fire-safety, while trust pages such as https://www.constructionconcern.com/contact and https://www.constructionconcern.com/about-us continue to outperform most service pages. That combination points to a July plan centered on search-aligned education, trust-building, and stronger bridges from reassurance into estimate intent. (Source: 2026-06 analytics review, July 2026; July research summary, July 2026)

June also clarified what not to overstate. LinkedIn produced 11 posts and 346 impressions but zero measurable engagement. Instagram generated 243 views, 73 reach, and a net -1 follower change. Threads remained low volume. Email delivered but did not generate strong click behavior in the visible June export. The July operating posture should therefore be pragmatic: use social for recognition, saves, and conversations, but let website behavior and search demand determine the priority topics. (Source: 2026-06 analytics review, July 2026)

## 1. What the Monthly Research Shows
July research shows that discoverability is being driven far more by direct traffic and Google organic search than by social referral. The strongest non-brand search lane continues to center on boxed eaves, fire protection, and related soffit or exterior-protection terms, while social traffic remains negligible by comparison. (Source: July research summary, July 2026; 2026-06 analytics review, July 2026)

### Key Findings
- Boxed-eaves and fire-protection content remain the clearest non-brand winner, led by https://www.constructionconcern.com/post/boxed-in-eaves-fire-safety. (Source: July research summary, July 2026)
- Trust pages outperform most service pages. https://www.constructionconcern.com/contact and https://www.constructionconcern.com/about-us are drawing materially more traffic than most individual service URLs. (Source: July research summary, July 2026)
- Social is still weak as an acquisition channel, which means July content should optimize first for recognition, saves, and estimate conversations instead of click volume. (Source: 2026-06 analytics review, July 2026)
- June LinkedIn content showed one useful direction despite zero engagement overall: myth-correction and whole-home logic outperformed generic credibility or seasonal reminder posts. (Source: 2026-06 analytics review, July 2026)
- June website behavior remained bounce-heavy, which means content has to answer homeowner questions quickly and clearly or visitors leave. (Source: July research summary, July 2026)

### Why It Matters
This means July should narrow rather than broaden the message. The account does not need more generic seasonal coverage. It needs more of the topics the audience is already proving it will search for, paired with stronger movement from education into service intent. The clearest play is to use search-aligned topics, practical diagnostics, and trust-building language to move people from top pages into deeper pages such as https://www.constructionconcern.com/request-free-estimate and relevant service pages. (Source: July research summary, July 2026)

## 3. Competitor and Market Signals
The current month did not include a fresh competitor packet, so the safest competitor picture is still the carried-forward one from prior research. American Vision Windows remains the visibility benchmark, Window World of Los Angeles remains the franchise-scale reference point, and American Deluxe remains the closest tone competitor. What changed this month is not competitor posture, but the evidence about what Construction Concern can win on: local trust, broader bundled scope, and search-aligned exterior-protection education. (Source: July research summary, July 2026)

### Competitor Observations
- American Vision Windows still has stronger public-proof density and larger omnichannel visibility.
- Window World of Los Angeles still competes through franchise polish, financing, and warranty framing.
- American Deluxe Windows and Doors remains the closest family-owned tone competitor, but Construction Concern still has the broader whole-home scope advantage.

### Market Implications
The most actionable market implication is search opportunity, not competitor movement. Non-brand impressions exist around boxed eaves, soffits, and local remodel terms, but many of those queries are not converting into clicks yet. That means July content should keep extending the proven boxed-eaves lane while also tightening local service-page relevance and clearer calls to action. (Source: July research summary, July 2026)

## 4. Seasonal and Local Context
July is no longer a preparation month in Southern California. It is a reaction month. The audience is already dealing with hot rooms, after-sunset heat retention, AC strain, and visible late-summer concern around exterior weak points. That changes both the tone and the urgency frame. (Source: July research summary, July 2026)

### Current Context
- Heat is showing up as a diagnostic problem: hottest room, hot glass, uneven cooling, and persistent evening warmth.
- Fire season is no longer an abstract awareness topic. It is an active exterior-check topic tied to eaves, soffits, siding condition, vents, and gaps.
- Timing pressure should point toward August, not toward spring-prep logic.

### Strategic Impact
Messaging should move from awareness into diagnosis. Generic summer reminders will not do enough work. Homeowners need practical explanations of what to inspect, why one-room patches often miss the bigger issue, and how exterior weak points relate to both comfort and fire-season relevance. (Source: July research summary, July 2026)

## 5. Strategic Recommendations for the Month

### Recommended Focus Areas
- Boxed eaves, soffits, and fire-season exterior preparation
- Mid-summer comfort diagnostics tied to windows and the home envelope
- One-window versus whole-problem planning logic
- Trust-building service education that leads naturally to estimate behavior
- Stronger movement from reassurance pages into https://www.constructionconcern.com/request-free-estimate and relevant service pages

### Recommended Content Priorities
- Let blogs and newsletters lead the monthly content system because they align best with search demand and trust-page behavior.
- Keep Instagram active with fewer, sharper, saveable diagnostic posts pulled from the strongest blog and newsletter topics.
- Use LinkedIn only for the sharper planning and myth-correction posts most likely to survive low engagement conditions.
- Keep Threads low-effort and repurposed.
- Keep newsletter cadence at two sends: one heat-diagnostic issue and one fire-season exterior-check issue.
- Push the four July blog directions already completed because they extend the strongest search lane and tie directly into the newsletter and social themes.

### Risks / Watchouts
- Bounce-heavy website behavior means weak or generic landing-page copy will continue to leak interest.
- If July email reporting again omits open rate, newsletter evaluation will remain incomplete.
- Overproducing social volume will spread effort too thin while the winning channels are elsewhere.

## 6. Channel Implications

### Social
Instagram should stay focused on mobile-readable, multi-point education because it is the best available format for saves. LinkedIn should stay narrow and selective. Threads should remain low-effort and observational. Social overall should amplify the best blog and newsletter themes instead of trying to operate as the primary discovery engine. (Source: July research summary, July 2026)

### Newsletter
Newsletter should remain a nurture and trust channel, not a click-heavy channel. Issue 1 should continue the July comfort-diagnostic theme. Issue 2 should continue the fire-season exterior weak-point theme. CTA language should continue to point toward a conversation or estimate rather than a hard conversion push. (Source: July newsletter file, July 2026)

### Blog
Blog remains the strongest strategic channel for discoverability. July should extend the four completed blog topics: one-room heat diagnostics, one-window myth correction, boxed-eaves fire protection, and exterior weak points before peak fire season. Priority URLs for traffic and internal-linking logic include https://www.constructionconcern.com/blog, https://www.constructionconcern.com/post/boxed-in-eaves-fire-safety, and https://www.constructionconcern.com/request-free-estimate. (Source: July blog file, July 2026)
