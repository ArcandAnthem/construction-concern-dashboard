# Client Dashboard - Construction Concern - July 2026

**Prepared by:** Arc & Anthem  
**Month:** July 2026  
**Status:** 🟡 Pivoting Toward Winning Channels

---

## Overview
The latest Wix and Later reports show that Construction Concern has stronger momentum in owned content and trust-building than in social distribution. That is useful news because it gives us a clearer growth path. The website, search visibility, the boxed-eaves fire-safety blog post, and the core trust pages are doing the heaviest lifting right now. July strategy should lean into those wins instead of asking every channel to perform the same job.

---

## 1. What Is Going Well

- Direct traffic and Google organic search generated 499 site sessions in the current visitor-source window, far ahead of social and email referral.
- The strongest content asset remains https://www.constructionconcern.com/post/boxed-in-eaves-fire-safety, with 163 views and 128 unique visitors.
- Trust pages are doing useful work. https://www.constructionconcern.com/ generated 296 sessions, https://www.constructionconcern.com/contact generated 99 sessions, and https://www.constructionconcern.com/about-us generated 74 sessions.
- The newsletter is still reaching the audience reliably. The June 30 send delivered to 523 recipients, and the May 26 send delivered to 529.
- Non-brand search demand is real around the fire-protection lane, including 127 impressions for `boxed eaves`, 110 for `boxed in eaves`, and 83 impressions plus 3 clicks for `boxed eaves fire protection`.
- Instagram still provides visibility even in a soft month, generating 243 total views in June while holding the audience at 364 followers.

## 2. What Needs to Be Corrected

- Instagram reach remained soft in June at 73 total reach, and follower count slipped by 1 over the month.
- Threads is currently a light-touch channel, producing 35 views and 4 likes in June with no replies, reposts, or quotes.
- Newsletter click behavior is weak. Both visible sends produced 6 unique clicks, which works out to about a 1.1% click rate.
- Newer blog topics have not matched the boxed-eaves winner yet. The June 29 fire-season checklist post produced 2 views, and the May 26 summer-heat windows post produced 9.
- Most traffic still stops at trust pages instead of moving deeper into service and estimate pages, which means the bridge from confidence to action needs improvement.

## 3. The Winning Strategy

### Clear Recommendation
Construction Concern should continue social media posting, but social should no longer be treated as the lead growth engine. The winning model right now is blog-led, newsletter-backed, and social-amplified.

### What That Means
- Blogs should carry the main educational and discovery work because search and blog traffic are the clearest non-brand win.
- Newsletters should turn the strongest blog ideas into trust-building nurture content and drive readers back to the most relevant website pages.
- Instagram and Threads should stay active, but they should mainly repackage the best blog and newsletter themes into short, saveable, practical posts rather than trying to function as primary traffic channels.

## 4. What Needs to Be Fixed

- Keep the content closer to proven topic lanes: boxed eaves, soffits, fire protection, hot-room diagnostics, older windows, and whole-home planning logic.
- Tighten newsletter calls to action so the next step is clearer and more specific.
- Make the pathway between https://www.constructionconcern.com/, https://www.constructionconcern.com/contact, https://www.constructionconcern.com/about-us, and https://www.constructionconcern.com/request-free-estimate feel more connected so trust-page traffic has a clearer conversion path.
- Use more real project photos and proof points wherever possible, because trust is already a strength and should be reinforced.

## 5. What Needs to Go

- Generic seasonal filler that is not tied to a proven homeowner problem or search signal.
- Social-first planning that assumes posts will drive most discovery on their own.
- Channel sprawl, where every platform gets equal energy even when the data says otherwise.
- Standalone posts that are disconnected from the stronger blog and newsletter themes.

## 6. Recommended Direction for July and August

- Lead the month with blog and newsletter topics in the fire-protection and heat-diagnostic lanes.
- Use social to echo those same themes with shorter educational hooks, proof points, and reminders.
- Prioritize content that moves from reassurance to action, especially from the home page, About Us page, and Contact page into Request Free Estimate and service pages.
- Measure success less by raw social activity and more by search visibility, blog traffic, trust-page engagement, newsletter response, and estimate-path behavior.
